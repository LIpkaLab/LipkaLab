Current Projects

  • Genomic Selection: Research in the Lipka Lab focuses on diversifying and refining genomic selection models to increase the accuracy of breeding value predictions. This includes integrating Genome-Wide Association Study (GWAS) results into GS frameworks as fixed-effect covariates, particularly for traits governed by both large-effect and small-effect genes. The lab’s work spans a variety of crops, including maize, sorghum, and wheat, with a strong emphasis on addressing genotype-by-environment ($G \times E$) interactions and exploring the "omnigenic model" to capture the subtle effects of peripheral genes across the genome.
  • Epistasis: The Lipka Lab investigates the role of epistasis—the interaction between different genes—to provide a more complete understanding of genetic architecture beyond simple additive effects. A key component of this work involves evaluating and refining statistical tools, such as the Stepwise Procedure for constructing an Additive and Epistatic Multi-Locus model (SPAEML), to accurately detect these non-additive signals. By quantifying false-positive rates and exploring phenomena like "phantom epistasis," the lab ensures that breeders can distinguish true biological interactions from statistical artifacts, leading to more robust characterizations of complex agronomic traits.
  • Machine Learning: The Lipka Lab leverages machine learning and artificial intelligence to enhance the predictive power of genomic models, especially when handling high-dimensional "omics" data. By evaluating the performance of diverse algorithms—from ridge regression to deep learning—the lab identifies the most effective methods for capturing the biological hierarchy of complex traits. To ensure these advancements are practical for the breeding community, the lab develops automated, user-friendly tools and R Shiny applications that streamline complex analyses, ultimately helping to "turbocharge" gene banks and accelerate the development of climate-resilient varieties.
